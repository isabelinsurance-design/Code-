## 1. MARIA MEDICARE — Medicare broker excellence (CMS-compliant)

### Thought leaders

- **Christopher Westfall** — Former law-enforcement officer turned full-time Medicare agent (2008), built MedicareAgentTraining.com which has trained "thousands of insurance professionals" via Senior Agent Podcast and his Medicare Supplement-by-phone curriculum; relevant because his playbook is built for solo phone-based brokers (Isabel's exact profile), not call-center floors. - **Ari Parker, JD** — Stanford Law graduate, co-founder of Chapter (a Medicare advisory firm), leads a team of 30+ licensed advisors, and authored the #1 bestselling Medicare consumer book *It's Not That Complicated*; he matters because his "3 Ps" framework (Premiums, Providers, Prescriptions) is a teachable consumer-facing simplification Isabel can adapt for Spanish-speaking seniors. [askchapter.org](https://askchapter.org/ari-parker) · [Kirkus](https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/ari-parker/its-not-that-complicated-the-three-medicare-decisions-to-protect-your-health-and-money/)
- **NABIP leadership (National Association of Benefits & Insurance Professionals, formerly NAHU)** — Industry body running the MMACR (Medicare, Medicare Advantage, and Compliance Requirements) 2026 certification, accepted by 51 carriers, costs $100, requires 85% pass score (vs AHIP's 90%), grants 8 CE credits in all 50 states. Authoritative because most carrier Isabel works with credential through NABIP or AHIP. [NABIP](https://nabip.org/) · [Ritter on 2026 NABIP](https://ritterim.com/blog/2026-nabip-medicare-certification/)

### Books, programs, frameworks

- **CMS Contract Year 2027 Final Rule** — Published April 6, 2026; effective June 1, 2026; marketing/communications provisions take effect Oct 1, 2026; coverage provisions Jan 1, 2027. The most consequential operational change: **CMS eliminated the 48-hour waiting period between SOA and personal marketing appointment**, effective June 1, 2026. SOA must still be agreed and recorded before the marketing conversation, and in-person appointments still require written SOA. SOA retention requirement: **10 years**, even if the client never enrolls. [CMS fact sheet](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/contract-year-2027-medicare-advantage-part-d-final-rule) · [Spark Advisors CY27](https://www.sparkadvisors.com/resource/cms-final-rule-2027-what-brokers-need-to-know) · [Informed+Choice on 48-hour rule](https://informedpluschoice.com/blog/cms-48-hour-soa-rule-walk-in-exceptions/)
- **2026 MA/PDP commission caps (CMS-set)** — Initial MA commission rose from $109 to $114/member/year (+4.6%); renewals from $55 to $57 (+3.6%); CA-specific rate rose ~10.8% YoY; **renewal still capped at 50% of initial FMV**; TPMO referral fees frozen at $100 MA / $25 PDP. The proposed flat-fee compensation regime and the contract-term restrictions were **stayed in July 2024 by Judge O'Connor and struck down on ratemaking-authority grounds** — they are postponed, not finalized. [Ritter on 2026 commissions](https://ritterim.com/blog/2026-maximum-broker-commissions-for-medicare-advantage-and-medicare-part-d/) · [Spark 2026 rule](https://www.sparkadvisors.com/resource/cms-final-rule-2026-what-brokers-need-to-know) · [Center for Medicare Advocacy on stay](https://medicareadvocacy.org/court-strikes-down-key-medicare-marketing-regulations/)
- **Chapter's "3 Ps" client decision frame** — Providers / Prescriptions / Premiums — taught to seniors as the only three filters they need. [askchapter.org](https://askchapter.org/ari-parker)
- **Ritter Insurance Marketing + Senior Market Sales (SMS) FMO playbooks** — Both are real, large, ethical FMOs; SMS recently acquired Senior Savings Network (2024 announcement) and is one of the largest senior-market distributors in the US; Ritter publishes the most-cited operator content (commission tables, certification guides). [Ritter](https://ritterim.com/) · [SMS acquisition](https://www.seniormarketsales.com/blog/senior-market-sales-acquires-senior-savings-network-in-latest-strategic-growth-move)
- **NABIP MMACR 2026** — Annual recertification curriculum + exam, replaces or supplements AHIP for many carriers, 5 free retakes. [Ritter on NABIP](https://ritterim.com/blog/2026-nabip-medicare-certification/)

### Actionable practices (specific protocols)

1. **Capture SOA before any plan-specific conversation, store ≥10 years** — including SOAs from clients who never enroll; auto-tag with date, scope (MA/PDP/MedSupp), and method (verbal-recorded, written, or electronic). The June 2026 elimination of the 48-hr wait means same-day enrollments are now compliant, but the *order* (SOA → discussion) is still binding. [Informed+Choice](https://informedpluschoice.com/blog/cms-48-hour-soa-rule-walk-in-exceptions/)
2. **Run a 12-month touchpoint cycle, not just AEP** — Anchor on each member's enrollment anniversary, not the calendar. Touchpoints: 90 days post-enrollment (welcome + drug-list confirmation), mid-year (provider check), August (pre-AEP setup), AEP review call (Oct–Dec). Drives retention and CMS-defensible "ongoing service."
3. **T65 pipeline starts 6 months before the 65th birthday** — ICEP window opens 3 months before and closes 3 months after; build a date-keyed automation that triggers at T-180, T-120, T-60, T-30. Industry standard for top producers.
4. **Use the 3 Ps script in Spanish first** — Doctores (Providers), Medicinas (Prescriptions), Costos (Premiums + MOOP). Parker's framework translates cleanly and matches how Latino seniors actually frame decisions. [askchapter.org](https://askchapter.org/ari-parker)
5. **Lean into familismo, don't fight it** — Latino healthcare decisions are family-network decisions, not individual ones; *familismo* describes supreme collective loyalty to extended family, and patients expect adult children to attend appointments and participate in decisions. Schedule first appointments for evenings/Saturdays when the hija/hijo can join. Plan: budget 90 min for first appointment vs the industry-standard 45. [Markkula Center](https://www.scu.edu/ethics/focus-areas/bioethics/resources/culturally-competent-care/culturally-competent-care-for-latino-patients/) · [NHCOA](https://nhcoa.org/healthcare-and-familismo-understanding-the-complexity-of-care/) · [PEAH on familismo](http://www.peah.it/2019/07/the-role-of-familism-in-latinx-communities-and-impact-on-health-care-decision-making/)
6. **NABIP-certify by June 30 annually** — $100, 85% pass, 5 free retakes, 8 CE credits, accepted by 51 carriers for 2026. [Ritter NABIP](https://ritterim.com/blog/2026-nabip-medicare-certification/)
7. **Track the "$500K broker" leverage points** — Retention (renewals are 50% of initial FMV but recur forever; a 95%+ retention rate compounds), referral velocity from family networks (familismo amplifies word-of-mouth), and AEP capacity (book-of-business size that can be touched in 8 weeks without burning out). The $500K vs $200K difference is mostly retention math + referrals, not new-lead spend.

### Red flags / avoid

- **Don't quote the old 48-hour SOA rule after June 1, 2026** — it's eliminated; agents who insist on it look outdated. [Informed+Choice](https://informedpluschoice.com/blog/cms-48-hour-soa-rule-walk-in-exceptions/)
- **Don't state CMS-set compensation caps as if final** — The flat-fee proposed compensation and contract-term restrictions were struck down for lack of ratemaking authority; only the consent (TPMO) requirements survived. Saying "CMS capped compensation at $X" without the litigation context is wrong. [Center for Medicare Advocacy](https://medicareadvocacy.org/court-strikes-down-key-medicare-marketing-regulations/) · [DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en/insights/publications/2024/07/cms-reverses-changes-to-agent-broker-compensation-rules)
- **Avoid the lead-gen call-center mindset** — Cold-call/spam-text lead vendors (the targets of TPMO regulation) tarnish ethical brokers. Westfall's training is explicit on this distinction.
- **Don't discuss plan specifics without a current SOA on file** — Even verbally. This is the #1 CMS compliance violation and creates real legal liability.
- **Avoid English-default forms for Spanish-dominant clients** — CMS requires meaningful access; Medicare.gov, all 8 of Isabel's carriers, and SOA forms are available in Spanish. Use them.

### What makes a TRUE Medicare expert

A true expert holds two things at once: (1) **operational mastery of the CMS calendar** — SOA workflow, 10-year retention, T65 ICEP, AEP Oct 15–Dec 7, OEP Jan 1–Mar 31, MMACR recertification — and (2) **clinical-adjacent literacy** — they can actually read a Summary of Benefits, compare MOOP, identify when a Medigap + standalone PDP beats an MAPD, and explain Part D's IRMAA brackets in plain Spanish. They retire the "captive carrier" mindset (selling only one plan because of comp) and run the formulary check + provider lookup in front of the client. The generic insurance coach can quote AEP dates; the expert knows that **the broker who doubles is the one whose 95%-retention book compounds faster than their AEP burst**, and structures every September around protecting that book rather than chasing new leads.

### Books, programs, frameworks

- **Profit First (Mike Michalowicz)** — Allocation: Profit 5–10%, Owner's Comp 30–50%, OpEx 50–60%, Tax 15–20%. For service businesses, the rule is to start at 1% if margins are tight then ramp, and to compute *Real Revenue* (gross − materials/subs) before applying percentages. [Michalowicz on percentages](https://mikemichalowicz.com/mastering-profit-first-master-your-percentage-allocations/)
- **The Simple Path to Wealth (J.L. Collins)** — Aggressive saving + VTSAX (total US stock market index) as the default; "F-You money" concept. Critique-aware: Collins explicitly contrasts the "simple path" with the "optimal path" and accepts a small alpha cost for huge behavioral gains. [Collins on simple vs optimal](https://affordanything.com/624-jl-collins-part-1-the-simple-path-vs-the-optimal-path/) · [Morningstar on Collins](https://www.morningstar.com/personal-finance/jl-collins-still-simple-path-wealth)
- **Get Good with Money (Tiffany Aliche)** — 10-step "Financial Wholeness" framework: budgeting, saving, credit, debt, learn-to-earn, investing, insurance, growing net worth, retirement, legacy. NYT bestseller. [Budgetnista about](https://thebudgetnista.com/about/)
- **Wealth Warrior (Linda Garcia)** — 8 steps tuned to communities of color, emphasizing the cultural barriers (familial money trauma, "don't risk it" generational messaging) that Spanish-language finance content rarely names. [Wealth Warrior book](https://www.amazon.com/Wealth-Warrior-Communities-Conquer-Market/dp/0306828480)
- **Solo 401(k) / SECURE 2.0 stack** — In 2026, Solo 401(k) cap is $72K under 50, plus $8K catch-up at 50+ ($80K total). Workers 60–63 get an enhanced catch-up to $83,500. **Critical rule**: If FICA wages exceeded $150K in prior tax year, all 2026 catch-up contributions **must be Roth/after-tax**. SEP-IRA does not allow catch-up at all, so Solo 401(k) dominates for any 50+ self-employed earner. [Fidelity 2026](https://www.fidelity.com/learning-center/smart-money/solo-401k-contribution-limits) · [SelfEmployed.com](https://www.selfemployed.com/solo-401k-contribution-limits-for-2026-how-much-self-employed-workers-can-save/) · [IRA Financial high-earner](https://www.irafinancial.com/blog/a-high-earners-guide-to-the-solo-401k/)

### Actionable practices

1. **Profit First with commission-burst adjusted percentages** — For an insurance agent with renewal residuals + AEP burst, set bi-monthly transfers from operating to (a) Profit 10%, (b) Owner's Comp 50% (smoothed across 12 months from the AEP burst), (c) Tax 25–30% (CA self-employment + federal), (d) OpEx remainder. The AEP cash spike funds the smoothed monthly draw. [Michalowicz allocations](https://mikemichalowicz.com/mastering-profit-first-master-your-percentage-allocations/)
2. **S-corp election once net profit clears ~$60–80K** — Insurance agents typically benefit from S-corp election to split W-2 salary (subject to SE tax) from distributions (not subject to SE tax); but the **Solo 401(k) employer contribution is calculated off W-2 wages only**, so under-paying yourself W-2 caps your retirement-deduction ceiling. [IRA Financial S-corp](https://www.irafinancial.com/blog/solo-401k-contribution-limits-2025-2026/)
3. **Quarterly estimated tax dance for CA + Federal** — Q1 (Apr 15), Q2 (Jun 15), Q3 (Sep 15), Q4 (Jan 15). Solo brokers underpay Q3 because Q3 hits *before* the AEP commission burst is received; safer to over-fund Q1 + Q4 (when AEP cash arrives) and aim for the safe-harbor 110% of prior-year liability if AGI > $150K. [IRS Pub 560](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p560)
4. **Front-load Solo 401(k) in Q1 from prior-year AEP cash** — Don't wait until December; deploying $80K (incl. catch-up) in January gives 11 extra months of tax-deferred compounding. [Fidelity Solo 401k](https://www.fidelity.com/learning-center/smart-money/solo-401k-contribution-limits)
5. **The retention/new-business ratio target** — For a Medicare book of 60–70 clients, sustainable income depends on >90% retention (renewals are CMS-set at 50% of FMV but recur 6–8 years per member). The math: 60 clients × $57 renewal × 90% = $3,078 *recurring* baseline, plus AEP-burst new business. The "abundance mindset" alternative ignores that compounding renewals are the actual wealth lever, not "manifesting" more leads.
6. **Use the Wealthramp / fiduciary filter when selecting a financial advisor** — Aliche launched Money Match with Wealthramp specifically to address bad fiduciary matches; the rule is fee-only, CFP, no commissions on AUM > 1%. [Wealthramp partnership](https://wealthramp.com/tiffany-aliche-the-budgetnista-presents-money-match-in-partnership-with-wealthramp-to-improve-the-financial-advisor-search/)
7. **Stack accounts in Patrice Washington's six-pillar order** — Health (Fit), relationships (People), home/space, faith, work-skills, money. The work-skills pillar comes *before* money because compounding income depends on compounding skill. [Six Pillars](https://patricewashington.com/redefining-wealth-podcast/the-six-pillars-of-wealth-how-to-use-them-to-get-fast-results/)

### Red flags / avoid

- **"Manifesting abundance" / frequency / Law of Attraction money advice** — Linda Garcia and Tiffany Aliche both teach in opposition to this; the data is index funds + savings rate + time, not mindset.
- **SEP-IRA for anyone 50+** — SEP doesn't allow catch-up contributions; on a $200K net the Solo 401(k) leaves $20K+ on the table vs SEP. [Fraim CPA comparison](https://fraimcpa.com/solo-401k-vs-sep-ira-which-is-better/)
- **Suze Orman's "you can't afford a latte" / shame-based budgeting** — Aliche's framing is explicitly that women, especially women of color, were not failed by spending but by lack of access; shame is the wrong primitive.
- **AUM advisors charging 1%+ on portfolios under $1M** — Mike Piper, JL Collins, and Aliche all converge here: at this asset level, a flat-fee CFP ($2–4K/yr) or a robo-advisor beats AUM by tens of thousands over a decade.
- **"Quit your job and go all-in" advice for a 53-year-old with established residuals** — The compounding Medicare book is the asset; volatility from a forced pivot is the enemy.

### What makes a TRUE finance expert (for this profile)

A true expert holds three uncomfortable truths at once: (1) **cash flow is the constraint, not net worth** — for a commission-based Latina solopreneur at 53, the question is "can the AEP burst smooth into 12 months without panic spending in March?", not "what's your retirement number?"; (2) **the tax code rewards structure, not hustle** — S-corp + Solo 401(k) + Roth backdoor + HSA, sequenced right, beats earning 30% more pre-tax; (3) **community-specific shame is real and operational** — Latina solopreneurs carry money trauma that "just invest in VTSAX" advice ignores. The generic coach quotes Dave Ramsey's debt snowball or sells a course; the true expert reads the prior-year 1040 + Schedule C, builds the quarterly-estimate calendar to the AEP cash curve, names the catch-up Roth requirement for high earners, and stops talking about "abundance."

## 3. VICTORIA VISION — Strategic planning + OKRs + 90-day rocks for a solo + small team

### Thought leaders

- **Gino Wickman** — Author of *Traction* (2007/2011), creator of EOS, which has been implemented in >220,000 companies; designed for 10–250 employees but **EOS founders themselves acknowledge it scales down to solopreneurs by stripping back to foundational components** (Vision, Rocks, weekly L10 meeting). For Isabel + 4-person team this is the right baseline. [EOS One blog](https://eosone.com/blog/book-summary-traction-by-gino-wickman/) · [EOS Ninety](https://help.eos.ninety.io/what-is-eos)
- **Greg McKeown** — *Essentialism* (2014) and *Effortless* (2021); his "top 25 list → top 5 → reject the other 20" exercise is the operational core of priority-stripping for solo operators. [Burkeman summary referencing McKeown](https://keithedwards.com/2022/01/03/6-lessons-from-four-thousand-weeks-by-oliver-burkeman/)
- **Oliver Burkeman** — Former *Guardian* psychology columnist, author of *Four Thousand Weeks: Time Management for Mortals* (2021); the framework is mortality math (average human life ≈ 4,000 weeks) used to force discrimination between commitments. For a 53-year-old running a service business, this is the philosophical foundation EOS is missing. [Burkeman's site](https://www.oliverburkeman.com/fourthousandweeks)

### Books, programs, frameworks

- **EOS / Traction (Wickman)** — 6 components: Vision, People, Data, Issues, Process, Traction. **Rocks** = 3–7 most-important things to accomplish in 90 days (the magic is "long enough to mean something, short enough to maintain urgency"); EOS shops report ~88–90% rock completion vs ~60–70% baselines. [FeedbackWrench summary](https://www.feedbackwrench.com/post/traction-book-summary-complete-guide-to-gino-wickmans-entrepreneurial-operating-system-eos) · [EOS Ninety](https://help.eos.ninety.io/what-is-eos)
- **Scaling Up / Mastering the Rockefeller Habits (Verne Harnish)** — 10 habits, ~40 behaviors, deployed by 102,000+ companies; the discipline is **adopt 1–2 per quarter** over 24–36 months, not all at once. Core ritual: weekly leadership meeting, quarterly Theme + Critical Number + 3–5 Rocks (Wickman borrowed the term from Harnish), Functional Accountability Chart (FACe). [Growth Institute](https://blog.growthinstitute.com/scale-up-blueprint/10-rockefeller-habits-checklist) · [Scaling Up coaches](https://coaches.scalingup.com/blog/blog-post-title-one-lpc9d-ewsb7-k3ryp-jz5xl-ddfh3)
- **Essentialism (Greg McKeown)** — "Discerning the vital few from the trivial many." The Buffett-attributed 25/5 exercise: list 25 priorities, pick top 5, *actively avoid* the other 20 because they will steal time from the 5.
- **Four Thousand Weeks (Oliver Burkeman)** — Reject the productivity-as-virtue frame; "perfectionism is the enemy of progress"; commit and settle, don't optimize. [Burkeman site](https://www.oliverburkeman.com/fourthousandweeks)
- **Building a Second Brain / PARA (Tiago Forte)** — Projects (active, deadlined), Areas (ongoing responsibility), Resources (reference), Archives. **Weekly Review** is tactical (process inbox, clarify next actions); **Monthly Review** is strategic (kill stale projects, re-rank areas). The weekly review is "what prevents the trusted system from decaying." [Tiago Forte blog](https://www.thesecondbrain.io/blog/how-to-implement-para-method-with-ai)

### Actionable practices

1. **Quarterly Rocks, not OKRs** — For a 5-person operation, OKRs become busywork; EOS Rocks (3 max per person, 1 company-wide Critical Number) give the same accountability with a tenth of the overhead. The 90-day cadence is the constant. [EOS One](https://eosone.com/blog/book-summary-traction-by-gino-wickman/)
2. **Weekly L10 meeting, same day/time, 90 minutes max** — Wickman's L10 format: Segue (5m), Scorecard (5m), Rock Review (5m), Customer/Employee Headlines (5m), To-Do List (5m), IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve — 60m), Conclude (5m). Even with 4 people, this beats Slack chaos. [FeedbackWrench](https://www.feedbackwrench.com/post/traction-book-summary-complete-guide-to-gino-wickmans-entrepreneurial-operating-system-eos)
3. **Friday weekly review using PARA** — 30 min: process inbox → projects, kill dead projects, set 3 priorities for next week. Tiago's monthly variant (last Friday of month) re-ranks Areas. [Second Brain weekly review](https://www.thesecondbrain.io/blog/how-to-implement-para-method-with-ai)
4. **The 25/5 quarterly cull** — At the start of each quarter, list everything pulling for attention (25 ideas), pick the 5 that become Rocks, and write down the 20 that get *actively rejected* — McKeown's point is that "no" without writing it down still consumes mental bandwidth.
5. **Mortality math at season planning** — Burkeman's reframe: at 53, Isabel has ~1,500 weeks left of an average lifespan. Each quarter is ~13 weeks ≈ <1% of remaining time. This forces the question: "is this Rock worth 1% of the rest of my life?"
6. **One-page strategic plan (OPSP)** — Harnish's framework: a single page with Core Values, Purpose, BHAG, 3–5 year targets, 1-year priorities, quarter Rocks, individual KPIs. For a 5-person team, the OPSP is enough — no separate strategy doc needed. [Rhythm Systems OPSP](https://www.rhythmsystems.com/blog/mastering-the-rockefeller-habits-one-page-strategic-plan-opsp)
7. **Adopt one Rockefeller habit per quarter, not all ten** — Harnish's explicit recommendation: ~24–36 month rollout. Trying to install all 10 at once is the #1 small-team failure mode. [Growth Institute](https://blog.growthinstitute.com/scale-up-blueprint/10-rockefeller-habits-checklist)

### Red flags / avoid

- **OKRs in a 5-person team** — Designed for Google-scale alignment problems; for a solo+4 team, the OKR ritual eats 10% of the working week with no marginal alignment gain over Rocks.
- **"Hustle harder" / "10X your output"** — Burkeman: the drive to get more done is itself an avoidance of the harder question (what actually matters). Reject any framework that doesn't force prioritization *by elimination*.
- **Trying to install full EOS at once** — Even Wickman's certified Implementers stage it; for a solopreneur start with Vision + Rocks + weekly meeting only.
- **More than 7 Rocks per quarter** — Statistical regression toward zero completion past ~5–7; the discipline is *fewer, not more*.
- **Quarterly planning that doesn't kill anything** — Adding without subtracting is the failure mode; the McKeown rule is that every new commitment displaces an existing one.

### What makes a TRUE strategic-planning expert

A true expert understands that for a 5-person operation, **the entire system fits on one page and runs on one meeting per week**. They reject framework worship (you do not need OKRs *and* EOS *and* Scaling Up *and* PARA — you need one ritual that actually happens). They know that 90-day cadences work because they survive both quarterly procrastination and quarterly panic; that 5–7 Rocks is the empirical sweet spot; and that the failure mode is not bad planning but *more planning than execution capacity*. The generic coach sells frameworks; the expert subtracts ruthlessly, names the 20 things Isabel is *not* doing this quarter, and runs the Friday review even when nobody feels like it.

### Actionable practices

1. **Pick one signature format, run it 12 weeks before iterating** — Latasha's accelerator structure is built around format consistency; YouTube's algorithm uses format signal (length, intro structure, B-roll pattern) as a retention predictor.
2. **Target ≥4% CTR and ≥50% retention on 5–10 min videos** — Below these, the problem is the hook, not the topic. Re-write the first 30 seconds before re-writing the rest. [Virvid AVD vs retention](https://virvid.ai/blog/average-view-duration-vs-retention-youtube-2026)
3. **Evergreen > topical for niche channels** — Roberto Blake's 2025/2026 thesis; for a Medicare educator, "How Medicare Part D really works" outlives "AEP 2026 updates." Evergreen compounds the back-catalog. [Creators Hub](https://youtubecreatorshub.libsyn.com/how-roberto-blake-thinks-about-youtube-in-2025-short-form-ai-and-creator-longevity)
4. **Welsh's tiered product ladder applied to a service business** — Free (YouTube + IG + newsletter) → low ($50 Spanish Medicare guide PDF) → mid ($200–500 group enrollment workshop) → high (1:1 Medicare consultation, already her core business). The free → mid path warms leads without commodifying the service.
5. **One long-form post or video per week, atomized into 5–7 short pieces** — Welsh's content multiplier: a single Saturday essay → 5 IG carousels + 2 YouTube Shorts. Beats trying to produce native content per platform. [Growth in Reverse](https://growthinreverse.com/justin-welsh/)
6. **Spanish-first content, English-second** — Isabel's audience is Spanish-dominant Medicare-aged Latinos, an underserved YouTube niche; the niche-down compounds (vs trying to compete in general personal-finance English).
7. **Bricia Lopez "second-act" pattern: build the CPG/IP layer on top of the service** — Lopez built mole + michelada products on top of the restaurant; Isabel can build a Medicare-guidebook + Spanish AEP-prep course on top of the agency. The IP outlives the AEP cycle. [Latina Powerhouse profile](https://www.hola.com/us/latinapowerhouse/20221004335624/bricia-lopez/)

### Red flags / avoid

- **Aesthetic-feed Instagram strategy for a 50+ service creator** — The "25-year-old aesthetic feed" model doesn't convert Medicare-age audiences; engagement on Reels + Carousels with face/voice does.
- **Going-viral chasing** — Roberto Blake explicitly warns: "attention ≠ value." Virality without retention is wasted impression spend.
- **Manifesting / "frequency" / spiritual-bypass brand content** — Linda Garcia, Daisy Auger-Domínguez, and Bricia Lopez all build on operational substance, not vibes; the audience is sophisticated enough to detect it.
- **Posting daily on every platform** — Welsh's empirical pattern is one anchor piece + atomization; daily-everywhere kills both quality and the underlying service business.
- **Niche-too-broad ("financial wellness for women")** — At 50+ the niche-down compounds; "Medicare for Spanish-dominant Latinas in SoCal" is the right altitude.

### What makes a TRUE brand expert for this profile

A true expert understands that **for a 50+ Latina service operator, content is a retention and referral asset, not a top-of-funnel lottery ticket**. They optimize for evergreen back-catalog, retention curves, and one-format consistency — not virality. They speak fluent algorithm (CTR ≥4%, retention ≥50%, hook-fixes the first 30 seconds) without making it the religion. They know that Welsh's tiered ladder beats Bricia Lopez's CPG playbook for an *information* service like Medicare, but both teach the same meta-lesson: the IP layer outlives the labor layer. The generic coach sells "build your personal brand"; the true expert names the signature format, sets the 12-week retention target, kills the second-favorite content idea on Friday, and protects the AEP operation from the content machine.
